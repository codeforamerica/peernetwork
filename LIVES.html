Developed in collaboration with the City of San Francisco, New York City, and Yelp, the Local Inspector Value-entry Specification (LIVES) is an open data standard which allows municipalities to publish restaurant inspection information to Yelp or any other website. And now, any city can participate.

Make the commitment to share your city’s restaurant health inspection scores, promote improved public health, and empower informed decision-making: pledge to implement the LIVES standard in your municipality.

Why Participate?

Empower Citizens

Make valuable health department information available to your residents in a consumer-friendly format so they can make more informed decisions. LIVES can help you share this important information with the 84 million unique visitors who use Yelp each month.

Improve Public Health

Transparency is good for public health outcomes. When the City of Los Angeles required that restaurants display hygiene grade cards on their entrances, studies found it led to a 13% decrease in hospitalizations due to food borne illness.

Drive Economic Development

Opening city data lets entrepreneurs build new products and services using that data, driving job creation and economic growth. Industry forecasts predict that governments that promote open data will foster 25% more private commercial activity in apps and services in 2013 than 2012.
